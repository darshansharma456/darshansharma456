Finding the peace in PanchPokhari

RPanch Pokhari is one of the main features of Langtang National Park, located in the Nuwakot, Rasuwa and Sindhulpalchok districts of the central Himalayan region. The Government of Nepal's Postal Services Department issued a stamp featuring Pokhari in 2011.

Panch Pokhara is situated at an elevation of about 4100m above sea level. This is a famous Hindu pilgrimage site in Nepal. Panch Pokhari trekking lies to the north of the Kathmandu valley; the chain of peaks called Jugal Himal that includes Dorje Lakpa (6966m) Madiya (6257m) and Phurbi Chhyachu (6,637 m). This is a remote and unfrequented region, despite being close to Kathmandu.


Panch Pokhari is a group of 5 Hindu holy lakes in the Sindhupalchowk District of Nepal. It is 9th highest altitude wetland in the world. Panch pokhari trekking begins from Chautara after drive 5-6 hours from kathmandu. we observe the different ways of life, culture and come in contact with the people in the small village, passing through the dense forestations and meadows. The top of Panch Pokhari (5 lakes) offers an excellent view of Gauri shanker , Dojre lakpa, Langtang Himalaya range, Jugal Himal range, Rolwaling range, etc. on the last day of the trip we take a bus ride back to Kathmandu from Melamchi for other adventure activities in Kathmandu.

Panch Pokhari trekking is non tourist trekking trail in Nepal. Panch pokhari ( five lake )trekking offers beautiful Himalayan view including the Gauri shanker, Dorje Lakpa, Langtang Himalaya range, Jugal Himalayan range, Rolwaling Himalayan range, Tibetan Himalayan range etc and pristine nature with district culture . Panch pokhari mean 5 holy ponds and Hindus religiously respect the place.
